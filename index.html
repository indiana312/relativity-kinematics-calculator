Non-accelerating lab reference frame

Scenario: A stationary observer in the lab watches a particle starting at rest get pushed by a constant continuous force along a straight line. Assume an idealized environment with no gravity. All measurements and calculations are from the perspective of the stationary lab observer. “a” is the particle’s constant proper acceleration, which is also the initial acceleration measured by the lab observer at the instant of launch (a = a₀).
